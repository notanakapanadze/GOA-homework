Animals

Animals are multicellular organisms that form the biological kingdom Animalia. They consume organic material, breathe oxygen, and can move.

  • Lions
  • Elephants
  • Tigers
  • Bears

Birds

Birds are warm-blooded vertebrates characterized by feathers, beaks, and laying hard-shelled eggs.

  • Eagles
  • Parrots
  • Penguins
  • Owls

Reptiles

Reptiles are cold-blooded vertebrates that usually have scaly skin and lay soft-shelled eggs on land.

  • Snakes
  • Crocodiles
  • Lizards
  • Turtles

Plants

Plants are mainly multicellular organisms, predominantly photosynthetic eukaryotes of the kingdom Plantae.

  • Trees
  • Flowers
  • Grasses
  • Shrubs

Bacteria

Bacteria are microscopic, single-celled organisms that thrive in diverse environments.

  • Escherichia coli
  • Streptococcus
  • Lactobacillus
  • Bacillus subtilis
