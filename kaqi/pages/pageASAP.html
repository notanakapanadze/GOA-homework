Information about A$AP rocky

Rakim Athelaston Mayers (born October 3, 1988), known professionally as ASAP Rocky (/ˈeɪsæp/ AY-sap; stylized as A$AP Rocky), is an American rapper. Born and raised in Harlem, he embarked on his musical career as a member of the hip hop collective ASAP Mob, from which he adopted his moniker. In August 2011, Mayers' single "Peso" was leaked online, and within weeks, began receiving radio airplay. He signed with Polo Grounds Music, an imprint of RCA Records in October of that year, and shortly after, released his debut mixtape, Live. Love. A$AP (2011) to widespread critical acclaim. Mayers' 2012 single, "Fuckin' Problems" (featuring Drake, 2 Chainz and Kendrick Lamar), marked his first entry-at number eight-on the Billboard Hot 100, received a nomination for Best Rap Song at the 56th Annual Grammy Awards, and preceded his debut studio album, Long. Live. A$AP (2013). Met with critical and commercial success, it debuted atop Billboard 200 and received double platinum certification by the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA). His second album, At. Long. Last. A$AP (2015), also debuted atop the chart and saw continued critical praise; its single, "L$D", earned a nomination for Best Music Video at the 58th Annual Grammy Awards. His third album, Testing (2018), peaked within the top five of the Billboard 200, while his fourth album, Don't Be Dumb, is scheduled for release in 2024. Mayers has won a BET Award, two BET Hip Hop Awards, an MTV Video Music Award Japan and an MTVU Woodie Award. His nominations include two Grammy Awards, six World Music Awards, three MTV Video Music Awards and two MTV Europe Music Awards. Furthermore, Mayers has worked in music video direction, as well as production or co-writing for other artists, often under the pseudonym Lord Flacko.


Personal life

ASAP Rocky, born Rakim Athelaston Mayers on October 3, 1988, in Harlem, New York City, has cultivated a multifaceted personal life that reflects his artistic persona and influences. Growing up in a challenging environment, Rocky faced the struggles of poverty and violence. His father was incarcerated when he was young, and his mother worked tirelessly to provide for the family, which included Rocky and his siblings. These experiences significantly shaped his worldview and the themes present in his music. Rocky was exposed to a variety of musical influences during his childhood, from hip-hop legends like Wu-Tang Clan and Tupac Shakur to rock bands like The Rolling Stones. This eclectic mix of genres played a crucial role in shaping his unique sound. He started rapping at a young age, joining the hip-hop collective A$AP Mob, which further fueled his ambitions. His breakout mixtape, "Live.Love.A$AP," released in 2011, showcased his lyrical prowess and established him as a prominent figure in the music industry. In addition to his music career, Rocky has ventured into fashion and modeling, reflecting his keen interest in style and aesthetics. He has collaborated with several high-profile brands and designers, further enhancing his public persona. His fashion sense often makes headlines, as he effortlessly blends streetwear with high fashion, challenging traditional norms. Rocky’s romantic life has also garnered significant media attention. He has been linked to various high-profile relationships, most notably with reality TV star and entrepreneur Rihanna. Their relationship, which began in 2020, has captivated fans and the media alike, as both are influential figures in their respective fields. Their public appearances together and social media interactions have made them a power couple in the entertainment industry. However, Rocky's life has not been without controversy. He has faced legal challenges, including a high-profile assault case in Sweden in 2019, which sparked international attention and discussions about racial discrimination and the treatment of artists abroad. This incident highlighted the complexities of his life as a celebrity, where personal choices can lead to significant consequences. Despite these challenges, Rocky remains a dynamic and influential figure. His ability to navigate the complexities of fame, fashion, and personal relationships while maintaining a connection to his roots speaks to his resilience. Rocky continues to evolve as an artist and public figure, balancing his creative ambitions with the realities of his personal life. His journey reflects not only his growth as a musician but also his ongoing exploration of identity, culture, and self-expression in an ever-changing world.

Songs

A$AP Rocky's music is known for its blend of East Coast hip-hop with Southern and Houston rap influences, as well as his unique, laid-back flow. He gained widespread attention with his breakout single "Peso" from his debut mixtape Live. Love. A$AP (2011), followed by "Purple Swag", which helped establish his sound and introduce him to the music industry. His first studio album, Long. Live. A$AP (2013), featured hit songs like "Goldie" and "Fkin' Problems"** (with 2 Chainz, Drake, and Kendrick Lamar), which quickly became chart-topping tracks. Rocky's second album, At. Long. Last. A$AP (2015), included singles like "L$D" and "Everyday", showcasing his experimental side with psychedelic and alternative influences, along with collaborations from artists like Rod Stewart and Miguel. With his third album, Testing (2018), Rocky continued exploring new sounds. Songs like "A$AP Forever" and "Praise the Lord (Da Shine)" (featuring Skepta) emphasized his willingness to experiment with new production styles and international influences. Rocky's music often combines introspective lyrics with creative beats, and he has continued to influence both the music and fashion industries with his artistic vision and evolving style.
