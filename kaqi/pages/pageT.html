Information about Tyler, the creator

Tyler Gregory Okonma (born March 6, 1991), known professionally as Tyler, the Creator, is an American rapper, record producer, director, and fashion designer. He has been cited as an influential figure in alternative hip hop during the 2010s. Okonma became well known in the late 2000s, when he emerged on the internet as the leader and co-founder of the music collective Odd Future. Within the group, Okonma participated as a rapper, producer, director and actor, releasing studio albums that he produced for its respective members. Okonma also performed on his sketch comedy show Loiter Squad (2012-2014). Along with his collaborations with the group, Okonma developed his solo career beginning with his self-released debut mixtape Bastard (2009). Bastard earned him recognition in the online music press as an emerging artist from the indie music scene. His debut studio album, Goblin (2011), brought him mainstream media exposure, aided by the popularity of the single "Yonkers" and its accompanying music video. During this period, Okonma faced controversy in the media for his horrorcore-influenced sound and his violent, transgressive lyrical content. After the release of his second studio album, Wolf (2013), Okonma began to separate himself from his horrorcore productions, turning to more accessible sounds including fusions of jazz, soul and R&B. In 2017, Okonma released Flower Boy, followed by subsequent albums Igor (2019) and Call Me If You Get Lost (2021), all of which earned him widespread critical acclaim and commercial success, with the latter two albums debuting at number one on the Billboard 200 and each winning Best Rap Album at the 2020 and 2022 Grammy Awards, respectively. His seventh studio album Chromakopia was released on October 28, 2024.

Personal life

Tyler, the Creator, born Tyler Gregory Okonma on March 6, 1991, in Ladera Heights, California, is a multifaceted artist known for his unique approach to music, fashion, and visual art. Growing up, Tyler faced several challenges that shaped his identity and artistic vision. He was raised primarily by his mother, with his father absent from his life. This dynamic instilled in him a sense of independence and resilience, which is often reflected in his work. Tyler attended the Arts Institute of Los Angeles, where he honed his skills in music production and graphic design. His early exposure to various artistic mediums played a crucial role in his development as an artist. In 2007, he co-founded the hip-hop collective Odd Future Wolf Gang Kill Them All (OFWGKTA), which gained significant attention for its unconventional style and controversial lyrics. This collective not only showcased Tyler's musical talents but also his ability to lead and inspire a group of like-minded individuals. Tyler's personal life has often intersected with his artistic endeavors. He has been open about his struggles with mental health, including anxiety and depression, which he frequently explores in his lyrics. His candidness about these issues has resonated with many fans, contributing to a broader conversation about mental health in the music industry. In addition to his music, Tyler is known for his fashion sense and has launched several successful brands, including Golf Wang. His designs often challenge traditional gender norms and reflect his unique perspective on identity and self-expression. Tyler's influence extends beyond music and fashion; he has also ventured into television and film, creating shows like "Loiter Squad" and producing the acclaimed animated series "The Jellies." Despite his public persona, Tyler values his privacy and has often kept details of his personal relationships away from the spotlight. He has been linked to various figures in the entertainment industry, but he tends to keep his romantic life relatively low-key. In recent years, he has spoken about his sexuality, indicating a fluid approach to love and relationships. This openness has been embraced by fans and has added depth to his character as an artist. Tyler's evolution as a person and artist is marked by his willingness to confront societal norms and challenge expectations. He has grown from a controversial figure in the hip-hop scene to a respected and influential artist known for his creativity and authenticity. Through his music and various artistic ventures, Tyler, the Creator continues to push boundaries and inspire others to embrace their individuality. His journey reflects a commitment to self-discovery and the importance of staying true to oneself, making him a compelling figure in contemporary culture.

Songs

Tyler, the Creator's music is known for its bold lyrics, inventive production, and genre-blending style. He first gained attention with dark, gritty songs like "Yonkers" from his album Goblin (2011), showcasing his rebellious style and unique voice. His third album, Wolf (2013), introduced a softer side, with tracks like "Awkward" and "IFHY" focusing on personal and emotional themes. With Flower Boy (2017), Tyler earned widespread acclaim, showing more vulnerability and growth in songs like "See You Again" and "Boredom", which combined hip-hop, soul, and jazz influences. The album addressed themes of loneliness, love, and self-discovery, marking a turning point in his sound. In IGOR (2019), he embraced a more experimental, synth-heavy sound. Songs like "EARFQUAKE" and "A BOY IS A GUN" tell a story of love and heartbreak, blending R&B, pop, and hip-hop elements. His latest album, Call Me If You Get Lost (2021), revisits classic hip-hop styles with hits like "WUSYANAME" and "LUMBERJACK", and explores themes of freedom, travel, and confidence. Tyler's songs reflect his artistic evolution, tackling complex emotions and blending genres, making him a unique and influential figure in modern music.
